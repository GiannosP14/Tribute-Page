Michail "Mikis" Theodorakis

The Greek composer and lyricist






Here 's a time line of Mikis Theodorakis life:


  • 1925 - Born in the Greek island of Chios on the 29th of July.
  • 1942 - Writes and releases his first poems.
  • 1943 - Settles in Athens and is teached by Filoktiti Oikonomidi.
  • 1947 - Gets arrested with the accusation of being sympathiser with guerrilla forces in the greek civil war.
  • 1949 - Gets released after beeing tortured on the island Makronisos.
  • 1950 - Appears at the theatre with his first work.
  • 1953 - Marries the doctor Myrto Altinoglou.
  • 1954 - Continues his music studies in Paris.
  • 1958 - Sets to music the first 8 verses of Giannis Ritsos "Epitafios".
  • 1959 - Receives the "Kopley" award for the best European composer of the year.
  • 1963 - Becomes President of the "Neolaia Labraki"
  • 1964 - Writes the famous "Syrtaki dance" for Michael Cacoyiannis film "Zorba the Greek".
  • 1967-1970 - Moves abroad, because of the dictatorship.
  • 1974 - Returns to Greece after the fall of the Colonels and continues his work.
  • 1981 - Serves as a deputy for the Communist Party until starting doubting the lefts. As a result he leaves the party in 1986.
  • 1989 - Returns to the politics, but this time for Konstantinos Mitsotakis.
  • 2019 - Gets hospitalized due to heart problems and underwents surgery later this year.
  • 2021 - Dies in Athens on 2nd September (96 years old), leaving behind him over 1000 songs.


I am not a hero. Heroes die young. I am just a citizen who does his duty.
-- Mikis Theodorakis
